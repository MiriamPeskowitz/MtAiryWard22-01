Heather

Cynthia Albrecht

I have been politically engaged all my life, but only recently have I thought about taking a role in the Democratic Party. Our party--both locally and nationally--needs to become more “small-d” democratic and start listening to it’s rank and file members. I would like to be part of that movement. As a history teacher with experience running a huge volunteer organization during Obama’s 2008 election, I believe I have the right skill set to make our division--historically one of the most engaged in the Ward 22--part of the new movement towards a more transparent and democratic Democratic Party.

About Wards and Local Politics

Wait, what's a ward? Exactly. It's a geographic division of a political party, or in other words, the smallest and most local political divisions we have. Philadelphia has XX wards, and each ward has a bunch of divisions. We vote by divisions. If you live south of Carpenter Woods and vote at Summit Church, the line on the left, your Ward is 22, division 1.

I know. I didn't know that either.

A Democratic Committee Person is the representative of our neighborhood’s interests within the Democratic Party.
